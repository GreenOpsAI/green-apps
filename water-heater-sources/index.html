Important Considerations for Your Home in Winter Park (Altitude 9,100 ft)

  • Our Cold Water Matters: Winter Park's tap water is significantly colder than the national average. This means every water heater has to work harder and use more energy. The dollar savings from a high-efficiency model like a heat pump or condensing gas unit are even greater here than the national average suggests.
  • High-Altitude Performance: Gas, propane, and oil water heaters must be certified for high-altitude operation to run safely and efficiently. An improperly configured unit will waste fuel and can be dangerous. Always confirm this with your installer.
  • Check Your Fuel Source: If you're unsure, check your utility bills. Do you pay for natural gas to "Xcel Energy," or do you have a large propane tank on your property? This is your starting point. If you only have electricity, the choice between a standard electric model and a heat pump is the most important financial decision you'll make.
  • Look for Rebates! Colorado and local utilities often offer significant cash rebates for installing high-efficiency heat pump or condensing gas water heaters. These can lower the upfront purchase price dramatically. Search for Colorado Energy Rebates Here

Ready to Make a Smart Choice?

Take these three simple steps to start saving money and ensure your home has reliable hot water.

1. Check Your Current Heater

Go look at your water heater. Note its age, fuel type (electric, gas, etc.), and size (gallons). If it's over 10 years old, it's time to plan for a replacement.

2. Review Your Utility Bills

See what you're actually paying for electricity (per kWh) or gas/propane (per therm/gallon).

3. Consult a Local, Certified Pro

A qualified plumber can assess your home's specific needs, discuss high-altitude requirements, and provide an accurate quote for your Winter Park home.
